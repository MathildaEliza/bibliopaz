Libraries for Peace
"Books instead of Weapons"


An initiative to transform the Colombian reality

Come and see

"Books Instead of Guns: Libraries as Sites for Peace in Colombia"

SALALM 2019, Austin, Texas, Friday, June 28, 10:30 am -- 12:00 pm.
A panel presenting this Colombian initiative:


There is no doubt that the role of librarianship and information science is an active force for change and social transformation in Colombia. A large number of Colombian librarians, in exercising their own profession, become, in turn, cultural activists who are actively helping to reverse a social and political reality that has weighed and continues to weigh on the development of all of Colombia.

There are innumerable local projects, initiatives and even simple meetings, in which Colombian librarians daily seek the shared strength to articulate solutions to problems that belong to the whole country. Jesus Alonso Regalado from SUNY-Albany and myself had the great fortune of being witnesses in situ when we participated in the last librarian workshop organized by FILBO in Bogotá where more than 100 Colombian librarians met and explained a wide range of initiatives.

Some of these Colombian projects are having great resonance outside their borders as they also help other Latin American and European countries to launch their own initiatives. Projects like the #BibliotecariosAlSenado movement that, since February 2017, promotes democratic access to information in Colombia--a movement that is "catching-on" with fury in other Latin American countries; or like the Historical Memory Center of the city of Medellín that is helping to turn what was considered the world capital of violence into an authentic beacon of Colombian culture.

It is precisely one of those projects that has great international resonance that we want to present here at the 2019 SALALM in Austin. The project that was crystallized at the beginning of 2017 with the name Libraries for Peace and coordinated by the National Library of Colombia, has become a world point of reference of how libraries can intervene in effective ways to mitigate the effects of violence in a society in conflict, as is the case of Colombia. The model has emerged thanks to a purely humanistic proposal.

With this web page we intend to offer you an introduction to this project of Libraries for Peace not only with the desire to illustrate this experience, but also to motivate your participation in the panel that we have organized for June 28 at 10:30 a.m.

Come and take part! You are all invited ...
Miguel Angel Valladares Llata
