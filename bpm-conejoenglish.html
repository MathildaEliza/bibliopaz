Gilberto Pabón Sandoval

Gilberto is a Librarian for Peace in the corregimiento of Conejo, Fonseca, La Guajira, Colombia
from February 26, 2017 to December 23, 2017,
and in the Los Robles La Paz municipality of the Cesar Department in the Valledupar
metropolitan area from February 13, 2018 to December 22, 2018.

    Gilberto tells us:
    "I am a cultural manager and promoter of reading with law studies at the Libre University."

    "My experience started in the Cultural Corporation Public Library Julio Pérez Ferrero, in the city of Cúcuta Norte de Santander. There I worked developing functions as a tutor and promoter of reading in the Municipal Reading and Writing Network of Cúcuta, led by the City Hall of San José de Cúcuta, which aims to promote reading and writing in children, youth, adults and seniors of the city of Cúcuta, with a particular focus on the populations in vulnerable positions regarding their rights.

    We managed, within the framework of this important and valuable initiative, to design library extension services for communities in different neighborhoods of Cúcuta, to create innovative activities to promote reading and culture, such as the "Lunadas Literarias" that were recognized as examples for the country by the Ministry of Culture of Colombia. We also were able to design educational and cultural activities around the book and cinema from the "Biblioexpress" (a mobile library that visited the neighborhoods and nearby municipalities of the city of Cúcuta).

    Also, I am the founder of the Cultural Corporation of Reading and Writing of the municipality Los Patios. I participated in the project of Mobile Public Libraries of the Ministry of Culture in 2017, I was also a librarian in the public libraries project for the paths of peace developed in 2018 by the Ministry of Culture.

    Currently I work as coordinator of the Cultural Corporation of Reading and Writing that is dedicated to creating strategic reading and writing plans with innovative character.

FARC building memory in Conejo

Where is Conejo?

The corregimiento Conejo is located in the municipality of Fonseca within the department of La Guajira. It has around 2500 inhabitants and is the largest district of the three that Fonseca has. It has very fertile land, so the main economy is agriculture and livestock. The production of coffee stands out and the Coffee Festival, celebrated every December for 16 years, is famous in the area. Due to its proximity to the Venezuelan border, Conejo was one of the main routes to Venezuela and it was also a region where the guerrillas were furnished with supplies. It was precisely in February 2016 when Conejo received all the international attention because this corregimiento was chosen by the FARC to make public its presence for the first time and to socialize with the troops stationed in this region.

Presentation of the project: "Public Libraries on the veredas and paths of peace"
May 15, 2018; (3 '56 ") "When the Mobile Public Library arrived at the hands of Gilberto" It came as a miracle," say its residents, a miracle that has brought together the entire community around books, tablets and computers. The School of Music Vallenata is a nursery where children and young people learn to play accordion, guitar, 'cajón' and guacharaca, In addition, with the help of a group of artists and the community, adorned the streets of the village in color with 20 murals that feature the history of the locality. "
National Library, September 2018.

"The Ministry of Culture indicated that one of the main projects, since the beginning of the library, was the recovery of the traditions of the town, among them, the love for vallenato. This project stems from the fact that in Conejo several years ago there was a group called the Dinastia Vallenata, which brought together some of the best talent in the area, however, the group had disintegrated and it was thanks to the library that some of its members and heirs of the tradition met again to compose and perform songs alluding to peace, to the people, and to what the library means to the community.

This resurgence of the group motivated the library and the municipal administration to adapt a space to create the Vallenata Music School of the Conejo Library, a space that has become a hotbed of new talent, where children and young people compose songs and learn to play accordion, guitar, cajón and guacharaca.

The sense of appropriation shared among the inhabitants of Conejo of its library is reflected in the streets of the corregimiento. A group of artists, with the support of the community, embodied the customs of their people in 20 murals that tell the history of the town, a story that, like a good vallenato, will say that one day a library arrived in their town and the community clung to her so much that she did not let it go. " El Pilón https://elpilon.com.co/

"In La Guajira, peace begins in a library" " headlines an article in the Pacifista, written by María Rodríguez, on October 8, 2018. Maria wrote this article to publicly acknowledge the awarding of the Daniel Samper Ortega National Prize for Public Libraries 2018 to the Rural Public Library of the Conejo' Corregimiento. The prize recognized this library as the best public library in the country. This article, which deserves to be read, tells how this library emerged from the evolution of the Mobile Library for Peace that started at the hands of Gilberto in the beginning of November 2016.
