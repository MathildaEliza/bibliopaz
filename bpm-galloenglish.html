Victor Solis Camacho

Librarian for Peace during 2017 in the village of Gallo
located in the municipality Tierralta in the Córdoba Department, Colombia.

    Victor tells us:

    "I am a peace librarian from Gallo village in Tierralta Córdoba, I am 27 years and was old born in Cúcuta - Norte de Santander. I am a social communicator and technologist working mostly in governmental agencies. I began working in 2014 as a promoter of reading in the municipal network of reading and writing of Cúcuta. During summer 2014, I was named director of the Simón Bolívar Public Library of the northern capital of Santander, where I have responsible for launching the so called "lunada literaria", which consisted of promoting reading and film among vulnerable populations in the countryside.

    In 2017 and 2018, as head of the Gallo Public Library, I became part of the group Librarians for Peace organized by the Ministry of Culture and the National Library of Colombia. Gallo was in the heart of the post-conflict land. Here I was responsible for launching different initiatives that allow moving "library equipment" to territories that are difficult to access. The "muloteca" and the "literary canoe" are two of those initiatives. In addition I was responsible for creating library and cultural content that has been well recognized by local users and has attracted national attention, as in the film series "Gallo as you had never seen it before - The series". These activities help the library to gain national recognition and to take fifth place in the Daniel Samper Ortega national library prize in 2018.

    I am pleased to see that The New York Times in Spanish noticed my work in Gallo, and highlighted it among the most important in the world: I am currently working at the Corpoculect, a Cultural Reading and Writing Corporation in the municipality of Cúcuta, the metropolitan area, and at the Venezuelan border, providing books, "lunadas", movies and libraries."

Where is Gallo?

The "vereda" Gallo is a rural area of the municipality of Tierralta, in the department of Córdoba. On June 23, 2016, Caracol Radio described what Gallo was like, just when it was chosen as the only site desinated by the Peace process for the FARC to concentrate in this part of the Colombian territory.

"To get to Gallo, one has to leave the urban area of Tierralta and travel an uncovered road for about 40 minutes; from there you arrive at the Port of Frasquillo, another township of the municipality, and take river transportation that navigates the Urra reservoir for about an hour to reach the final path

When the canoe reaches the shore, the first thing you can see are two huts with palm trees and a board fence, each one separated from the other by more than 5 meters.

On entering the village, the panorama reveals that it is a sparsely populated area, which is confirmed by its inhabitants, who say that the community is made up of 31 houses and 152 people, including 60 children.

In Gallo all the proceedings must be done before noon, since at eleven o'clock in the morning the last canoe leaves from the "vereda" of Frasquillo headed to this "vereda". Food is bought weekly to save money on tickets; each person pays 32 thousand pesos round trip and the canoes can carry 15 passengers. If an inhabitant of Gallo, for any reason stays in Frasquillo after last canoe departs, he must pay about two hundred thousand pesos to be expressly taken to his community".


 

The job



 

With books, technology, games, crafts, digital literacy services and cineforos, #BibliotecarioDeLaPaz Victor Solis Camacho arrived with his program "Muloteca Viajera", and began opening educational and cultural paths along the inhospitable trails of Alto Sinú, in Tierralta, Córdoba.

The mules arrived in canoes and crossed the mountains and invited children and adults to participate in the activities of library extension that arrived with the "Traveling Library", and that took place in the indigenous La Mina council.

The "literary canoe" and the "muloteca"

The influence of the Public Library for Peace was extended to the indigenous communities thanks the "literary canoe" and the "muloteca".



"Gallo Let's make movies"
 
One of the activities that had the most impact within the community was the organization of technological training workshops (appropriation) requested by the users and organized by Victor. These included not only workshops on the use of the computer and the Internet, but also advanced film making workshops were offered. Thanks to these workshops, the community could work on describing their path by using video and documentary as a channel of collective expression throughout the region.
